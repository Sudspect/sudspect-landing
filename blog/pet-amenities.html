The 7 Best Pet Amenities for Apartment Communities in 2026

Why Pet Amenities Are No Longer Optional

Pet ownership in the United States has never been higher. According to the American Pet Products Association, 70% of U.S. households own at least one pet — and among renters, that number climbs even higher. When prospective residents search for an apartment, pet-friendliness ranks among the top five deciding factors. A "pets allowed" policy is the baseline. The communities that win lease signings — and more importantly, renewals — are the ones that go a step further.

Pet amenities signal something important to residents: you thought about their lifestyle, not just their rent check. That translates directly into satisfaction scores, word-of-mouth referrals, and lower turnover costs. The National Apartment Association estimates that a single unit turnover costs a property between $3,000 and $5,000 once you account for vacancy, cleaning, and re-leasing fees. A well-chosen pet amenity can pay for itself many times over just by retaining two or three households per year.

Below are the seven pet amenities generating the most resident interest heading into 2026 — along with realistic cost ranges and what each one means for your community.

1. Dog Park or Off-Leash Area

What it is: A fenced outdoor space where residents can let dogs run freely without a leash. Even a modest 2,000–4,000 square foot area can make a significant difference for dog owners living in smaller units.

Cost range: $5,000–$30,000 depending on size, fencing type, turf, shade structures, and waste station integration.

Resident impact: High. Dog owners cite off-leash exercise as one of their top unmet needs in apartment living. Communities with dedicated dog parks consistently score higher on resident satisfaction surveys and attract dog owners who are willing to pay a premium for the feature.

2. Pet Spa or Full-Service Grooming

What it is: An on-site or partnership-based grooming salon offering services like baths, haircuts, nail trims, and ear cleaning — available to residents by appointment.

Cost range: $20,000–$80,000 for a built-out spa suite; third-party partnership models can reduce or eliminate upfront costs.

Resident impact: High convenience value, especially for residents who work long hours. The downside is scheduling friction — appointments book up, and not all residents want a full grooming service for routine maintenance between cuts.

3. Pet Waste Stations

What it is: Dispenser units placed throughout the property that provide waste bags and a covered disposal bin. A foundational amenity at this point — most Class A and Class B communities already have them.

Cost range: $150–$400 per station installed; most properties need 8–15 stations depending on size.

Resident impact: Moderate individually, but strong in aggregate. Residents without dog waste stations reliably cite it as a frustration. Some communities are adding DNA-based waste tracking programs to enforce compliance — these run $1,500–$3,000 per year but can dramatically reduce complaints.

4. On-Site Dog Wash Station

What it is: A self-service bathing unit installed in a utility room, garage, or dedicated pet wash space. Dogs are secured in a tub with a professional-grade spray wand, shampoo, conditioner, and drying tools — all without the hassle of flooding your own bathroom.

Cost range: Traditional plumbed-in stations range from $3,000–$10,000 installed. Newer autonomous models eliminate that capital cost entirely through a revenue-share or zero-cost placement model.

Resident impact: Very high — and growing. Self-service dog washing sits at the intersection of convenience, affordability, and frequency. Most dogs need a bath every two to four weeks; a full-service groomer appointment costs $60–$120 and requires advance scheduling. An on-site station lets residents handle routine maintenance in 15–20 minutes at a fraction of the cost.

5. Pet-Friendly Common Areas

What it is: Designated indoor or covered outdoor spaces where residents can socialize with their pets — think a covered patio with water bowls, seating, and weather protection, or a pet-friendly lounge near the clubhouse.

Cost range: $2,000–$15,000 depending on scope and existing infrastructure.

Resident impact: Moderate to high. Pet-friendly common areas build community as much as they serve pets. Residents who meet their neighbors because of a shared space tend to stay longer and feel more connected to the property.

6. Pet Concierge Services

What it is: Concierge-style coordination of pet services — dog walking referrals, veterinarian partnerships, pet-sitting connections, or a dedicated app feature that lets residents manage pet-related requests.

Cost range: $500–$5,000 per year depending on whether it's app-based or staff-supported.

Resident impact: A differentiator at the luxury end of the market. Residents in Class A communities increasingly expect lifestyle services, and pet concierge bridges the gap between a standard amenity package and a true pet-welcoming environment.

7. Veterinary or Pet Health Partnerships

What it is: A formal referral relationship or on-site visit program with a local veterinary clinic, mobile vet, or telehealth pet service. Some properties host monthly mobile vet pop-ups.

Cost range: Often low to no cost — many local practices will partner at no charge in exchange for referral traffic. On-site clinic buildouts are in the $20,000–$100,000+ range.

Resident impact: High perceived value, especially for new pet owners and seniors with pets. Even a simple preferred-partner card at move-in creates meaningful goodwill.

Choosing the Right Mix for Your Community

Not every amenity makes sense for every property. The right combination depends on your resident demographics, available square footage, and budget. As a general rule:

  • Start with the basics: Waste stations and a pet-friendly policy are table stakes.
  • Add high-frequency amenities next: Features residents use every week — like a dog wash station — deliver more sustained value than features used occasionally.
  • Layer in community builders: Dog parks and pet-friendly common areas strengthen retention because they create habits and social connections tied to your property specifically.
